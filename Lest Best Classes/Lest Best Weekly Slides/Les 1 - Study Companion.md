## 6. Zinsbouw, Sentence structure

Voorbeeldzin, example sentence.

| Nederlands | English |
|---|---|
| Zullen we morgen om 16:00 uur vergaderen? | Shall we have a meeting tomorrow at 16:00? |

Wat valt op, what stands out.

The finite modal verb "zullen" jumps to position one because the sentence is a yes-no question. The main verb "vergaderen" stays at the end, in the infinitive. This is the modal verb bracket, the so-called "tangconstructie", finite verb early, infinitive at the end.

Pattern, finite verb, subject, time, place, infinitive.

## 7. Modale werkwoorden, Modal verbs

The five Dutch modal verbs.

| Werkwoord | Meaning, betekenis |
|---|---|
| kunnen | can, to be able to |
| mogen | may, to be allowed to |
| willen | to want |
| zullen | shall, will (often a proposal) |
| moeten | must, to have to |

Present tense conjugations.

| Persoon | kunnen | mogen | willen | zullen | moeten |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ik | kan | mag | wil | zal | moet |
| jij, je | kan, kunt | mag | wil | zal, zult | moet |
| hij, zij, het | kan | mag | wil | zal | moet |
| wij, we | kunnen | mogen | willen | zullen | moeten |
| jullie | kunnen | mogen | willen | zullen | moeten |
| zij, ze | kunnen | mogen | willen | zullen | moeten |

Function and example.

| Functie, function | Voorbeeld, example | English |
|---|---|---|
| Capaciteit, capacity | Ik kan goed dansen. | I can dance well. |
| Mogelijkheid, possibility | Kan ik pinnen? | Can I pay by debit card? |
| Toegestaan, permitted | Mag je hier roken? Ja, dat mag. | May you smoke here? Yes, that is allowed. |
| Niet toegestaan, not permitted | Nee, dat mag niet. | No, that is not allowed. |
| Instructie, instruction | Je mag hier even wachten. | You may wait here for a moment. |
| Wens, wish | Ik wil goed Nederlands leren. | I want to learn Dutch well. |
| Voorstel, proposal | Zal ik koffie halen? | Shall I get coffee? |
| Verplichting, obligation | Je moet op tijd komen. | You have to be on time. |
| Sterk advies, strong advice | Je moet deze soep eens proberen. | You really should try this soup. |
| Iets wat nodig is, something needed | Hij moet een grotere maat hebben. | He needs a bigger size. |

Belangrijke uitzondering, important exception.

The negative of "moeten" is not "moet niet" but "hoeven niet, te". "Moet niet" means "must not", a prohibition, while "hoeft niet te" means "does not need to".

| Nederlands | English |
|---|---|
| Je moet op tijd komen. | You have to be on time. |
| Je hoeft niet op tijd te komen. | You do not need to be on time. |
| Je mag niet roken. | You are not allowed to smoke. |

## 8. Klemtoon, Word stress

The underlined syllable is stressed. Dutch stress is not always on the first syllable, especially in loanwords and longer derivations.

| Woord | Klemtoon | English |
|---|---|---|
| Universiteit | universi-TEIT | university |
| Afdeling | AF-deling | department |
| Faculteit | facul-TEIT | faculty |
| Overleg | over-LEG | meeting, consultation |
| Vergadering | ver-GA-dering | meeting |
| Onderzoeker | onder-ZOE-ker | researcher |
| Student | stu-DENT | student |
| Wetenschap | WE-tenschap | science |
| Normaal | nor-MAAL | normal |
| Automaat | auto-MAAT | machine, vending machine |
| Project | pro-JECT | project |
| Werkgever | WERK-gever | employer |
| Betalen | be-TA-len | to pay |

Vuistregels, rules of thumb.

Words ending in "-teit", "-aal", "-eel", "-aan", "-ent", "-eur" usually carry stress on the last syllable. Native compound nouns like "werkgever" and "wetenschap" usually carry stress on the first element. Verbs with the unstressed prefix "be-", "ge-", "ver-", "ont-", "her-" never take stress on that prefix, the stress falls on the next syllable, hence "be-TA-len", "ver-GA-deren".
